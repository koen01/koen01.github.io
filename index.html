01 / About

A short note

I'm Koen — a hobbyist developer who likes the kind of problems that live half in code and half on a workbench. I'm happiest when there's a soldering iron warming up next to my laptop and a 3D print quietly humming somewhere in the room.

Most of my projects start small: a missing feature on a printer, a thing my home should obviously do on its own, an app I wished existed. I build them for myself first, then put them up in case they're useful to anyone else.

This site is a small index of those projects, written out in plain language.

  • i.

    3D printing

    Mostly Creality K1-series with a CFS unit. Fascinated by the unglamorous middle layer — spool tracking, slicer plumbing, RFID tags.

  • ii.

    Home automation

    Every blinking LED in the house is a future integration. I tinker with sensors, dashboards, and small bridges between things that don't want to talk to each other.

  • iii.

    Software

    Web dashboards, Android apps, Flutter prototypes. Whatever shape the problem needs. I like small tools that do one thing well.

Things I've built

→ 001
CFSync Web WebSocket 3D printing

A local web dashboard for managing filament spools on Creality K1-series printers with the CFS unit (K1, K1C, K1 Max, K1 SE, K2 Plus). Connects directly to the printer over WebSocket, reads live spool data from every CFS slot, and optionally syncs consumption back to Spoolman.

 → 002
CFtag Android RFID 3D printing

An Android app for reading and writing Creality RFID filament spool tags. Pairs with CFSync and Spoolman to give you a seamless, end-to-end filament tracking workflow — from the spool in your hand to the slot in the printer.

 → 003
Moonraker Viewer Flutter Moonraker Read-only

A super-simple Flutter app that puts your 3D printer's camera feed fullscreen with live print info overlaid — landscape mode, pinch-to-zoom, no buttons to press. The lazy person's print monitor, sitting on a stand by the bench.

Now

Iterating on CFSync, learning the messy parts of RFID, and slowly automating one more corner of the house.
